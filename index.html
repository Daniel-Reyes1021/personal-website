Daniel A. Reyes

• Coder • Pianist • 1/3 of ScholarlySiblings • Gym Enthusiast

I am a community-based entrepreneur, web designer, and musician passionate about building accessible, empowering technology for underserved communities. As a founder of ScholarlySiblings, I’m inspired by our mission to equip low-income first-generation and immigrant students with the tools, resources, and confidence to explore new horizons, define their own paths, and embrace their artistic selves.

With three years of experience in computer science, I’ve applied my skills to design the Milne Public Library’s website using HTML and CSS, while also teaching students ages 10–14 how to code in those languages over a 5-week program. I’ve taught math, directed music productions for youth, and developed grant proposals that directly benefited community nonprofits.

Now, as a double major in Computer Science and Music at Williams College, I’m continually exploring ways to merge my technical expertise with artistic expression and social impact. Whether I’m building web platforms, playing piano for the Williams Christian Fellowship, or creating data-driven solutions for youth programs, my work is rooted in service, creativity, and the belief that knowledge should be accessible to all.

Outside of work, you’ll often find me composing music, performing, or collaborating with peers on projects that connect technology, education, and the arts.
